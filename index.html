Food & Compliance Related

APEDA

Requirements:
  • Business Identity Proof: Certificate of Incorporation / Partnership Deed / Proprietorship declaration / LLP Agreement
  • PAN card of company/firm/individual
  • Import Export Code (IEC) (issued by DGFT – mandatory for APEDA)
  • Bank Details: Canceled cheque (with IFSC code & account number visible) and Bank Certificate (on official letterhead)
  • Address Proof of Business Premises: Electricity bill / Rent agreement / Sale deed
  • Contact Details of Authorized Person: Mobile number, email ID, photograph
Timeline: Registration certificate is generally issued within **7–10 working days** after successful submission & verification.
Costing: One-time registration fee: **₹5,000 + GST (18%)**. No annual renewal; only modification charges if you change company details.
Where to Apply: Online via the official **APEDA website → apeda.gov.in**. Application is done through the DGFT / e-RCMC portal using your IEC number.

ISO

Requirements:
  • Company registration certificate (Partnership Deed, Incorporation Certificate, GST Certificate, etc.)
  • PAN card of the business
  • Address proof of office (electricity bill, rent agreement, or NOC from owner)
  • Organizational chart and list of employees
  • Company profile & scope of business
  • Quality Manual & Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
  • Proof of past audits (if applicable)
  • Purchase bills, sales bills, balance sheet (for relevant standards)
  • Training records of employees
Timeline: 3 to 6 months with thorough preparation.
Costing: ₹4,000–₹10,000 for ISO 9001. Specialized standards cost ₹15,000–₹50,000.
Where to Apply: Through accredited certification bodies (NABCB or IAF-accredited).

Trademark

Requirements:
  • Applicant details: Name, Address, Nationality
  • Business registration proof (Partnership Deed, Certificate of Incorporation, MSME Certificate, GST)
  • Logo / Brand name (soft copy, if applicable)
  • Signed Power of Attorney (Form TM-48) if filed by agent/attorney
  • Identity proof of applicant (Aadhar, PAN, Passport, etc.)
  • Address proof of applicant (Electricity Bill, Driving License, etc.)
  • Class under which trademark is sought (Nice Classification list)
Timeline: Typically spans 12–18 months. Expedited processing can take as little as 3 months.
Costing: Online filing fees are ₹4,500 (individuals) and ₹9,000 (companies) per class.
Where to Apply: Via the IP India portal.

GS1 India Certification

Requirements:
  • PAN card of the company/owner
  • GST Registration Certificate
  • MSME/Udyam Registration (if applicable, for fee concession)
  • Incorporation Certificate / Partnership Deed / Shop & Establishment Certificate
  • Bank Certificate or cancelled cheque
  • Product list with categories
  • Contact details of authorized signatory
Timeline: Issued within 3–10 working days.
Costing: Based on annual turnover. Example: Up to ₹1 crore, license fee is ₹3,000 with a ₹750 annual renewal.
Where to Apply: Online via the GS1 India “Get Barcodes” portal.

IEC Code

Requirements:
  • PAN card of individual/company/firm
  • Aadhaar card or Voter ID/Passport/Driving License of applicant
  • Incorporation Certificate / Partnership Deed / Trust Deed (depending on entity type)
  • Bank details: Cancelled cheque & Bank Certificate (ANF-2A format)
  • Address proof of business premises (Rent agreement, electricity bill, sale deed, etc.)
  • Digital Photograph of applicant (passport size, JPEG format)
  • Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) of applicant (Class III, for companies/LLPs)
Timeline: Issued within 10–15 days.
Costing: Processing fee is ₹500. Annual updates are mandatory.
Where to Apply: Through the DGFT’s IEC Profile Management portal.
Funding Related

Startup India Seed Fund

Requirements: DPIIT-recognized startup; may need incubator endorsement.
Timeline: Applications year-round; funds disbursed based on milestones.
Where to Apply: Via Startup India portal or incubator network.
Amount: Grants up to ₹20-50 lakhs.

Other State Seed Funds

Details: Schemes available in states like J&K, Gujarat, and Rajasthan.
Amount: Varies by state; for example, J&K offers up to ₹10 lakhs.

Mudra Loan

Requirements: No collateral, no processing fees; for micro-units.
Timeline: Typically shorter process.
Where to Apply: Banks, MUDRA-affiliated NBFCs.
Details: Loans up to ₹20 lakhs for repeat borrowers; categories: Shishu, Kishor, Tarun.

Stand-Up India

Requirements: For SC/ST and women entrepreneurs who are starting a new ("greenfield") enterprise.
Loan Range: The loan amount can range from ₹10 lakhs to ₹1 crore.
Where to Apply: You can apply at participating banks that are part of the scheme.

Credit Guarantee Schemes

Details: These schemes enable MSMEs to get collateral-free credit. The government body acts as a guarantor for the loan.
Budget 2024 Scheme: The Budget 2024 Scheme offers a guarantee of up to ₹100 crore and has also doubled the Mudra limit for repeat borrowers to ₹2 million.
